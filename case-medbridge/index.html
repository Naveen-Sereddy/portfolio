UX Case Study · Healthcare

MedBridge

An end-to-end patient-portal redesign: appointments, prescriptions, records, and billing unified into one calm, secure experience.

My role
Research · IA · UI · Design System
Platform
Responsive web & mobile
Timeline
Concept project · 2024
Outcome
5 core flows unified

The problem

Healthcare portals are often hard to navigate, especially when patients manage appointments, prescriptions, medical records, and provider communication across multiple screens and workflows.

Goals

Unify the core patient flows into one place. Improve navigation, discoverability, and accessibility. Reduce friction and raise task completion, while staying understandable for users with very different levels of technical literacy.

01

Discovery · Research

User personas

Two archetypes anchor every design decision: a busy, mobile-first patient and an older caregiver. Each trade-off traces back to a real person and their goals.

02

Discovery · Research

Journey map

Mapping the appointment journey surfaced the exact moments patients hesitate or drop off, and the opportunities to remove that friction.

03

Discovery · Research

Information architecture

A flat, predictable structure so patients reach appointments, records, and billing without hunting through nested menus.

04

Design · Wireframes

Low-fidelity wireframes

Grayscale wireframes settle layout and hierarchy before any visual design: fast to change, cheap to test.

05

Design · Wireframes

Mid-fidelity wireframes

Real content and component structure validate the flows before committing to final UI.

06

Design · System

Design system

A healthcare-focused system keeps the product consistent and accessible: color roles, a type scale, a 4px spacing rhythm, and the core components every screen is assembled from.

Key decision

Balance clinical information density with simplicity. Patients need a lot of information, but the interface has to stay understandable across very different levels of technical literacy.
07

Design · Accessibility

Accessibility audit

Built to WCAG 2.1 AA: contrast-checked color, a logical keyboard path, descriptive screen-reader labels, and touch targets that clear the 44px minimum.

08

Deliver · Final UI

Final UI

The unified portal, fully designed and responsive: one information architecture that adapts from a focused desktop workspace to a thumb-friendly mobile app.

09

Deliver · Prototype

Clickable prototype

The primary path, clickable end to end across 30 real screens: sign in → dashboard → book → pick date & time → confirm → success. Real screen-to-screen navigation, not a slideshow.

The result

A unified patient portal that simplifies healthcare interactions while improving navigation, accessibility, and information discoverability, designed to hold up for the least technical patient in the room.
